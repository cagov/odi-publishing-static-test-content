Discrimination

  • Discrimination Complaints

    Discrimination is the act of making distinctions between people based on the groups, classes, or other categories to which they belong or are perceived to belong. People may be discriminated on basis of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation, as well as other categories. Complaints of discrimination may be referred to the CA Civil Rights Department (formerly the CA Department of Fair Employment and Housing).

Environmental

  • Pesticide Violations

    Pesticide Violation allegations are handled by the local county Agricultural Commissioner’s Office.  To find your local ag commissioner please visit the CDFA Website.

    For more information on pesticide related claims please visit the California Department of Pesticide Regulation.

  • Odor, Light or Noise Complaints

    Odor nuisance, light pollution or noise complaints are generally handled by your local city or county (depending on the local jurisdiction) Code Enforcement Department. 

    Find your county’s website information here:  https://www.counties.org/county-websites-profile-information. Or find your city’s website information here: https://www.counties.org/cities-within-each-county

  • Wildlife Complaints

    Negative impact of on wildlife allegations are handled by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife. Members of the public remain our best source of information related to illegal cannabis cultivation. If you witness a polluting or poaching incident, a fish and game code violation, or have information about a violation, call CalTIP at 1-888-334-CALTIP (888-334-2258) or https://wildlife.ca.gov/Enforcement/CalTIP 

Housing

Employment and taxes

  • Payroll or Employment Tax Complaints

    Payroll or Employment Tax matters are referred to the California Employment Development Department (EDD). Learn more about how to report fraud on the EDD website.

  • Cannabis Excise or Sales Tax Complaints

    Cannabis Excise or Sales Tax matters are referred noted and referred to the California Department Fee and Tax Administration (CDTFA).  Learn how to report suspected tax fraud activity on thee CDTFA website.

  • Identity Theft Reports

    Report identity theft to the CA Office of the Attorney General.

Returns, refunds, exchanges

  • Returns, Refunds or Exchanges

    Consult the Local Better Business Bureau (BBB) (www.bbb.org) and local consumer agency (which can be found DCA’s website as they have a list of all consumer agencies by county in CA). View the full list of dispute resolution programs on their website.

Scams

  • Identity Theft Reports

    Report identity theft to the CA Office of the Attorney General.

  • Fraud Reports

    Fraudulent use of a person’s address on a website can be reported to the State of California Department of Justice using their online form for consumer complaints against a business or company.
