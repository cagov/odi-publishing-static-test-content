Discrimination

  • Discrimination Complaints

    Discrimination is the act of making distinctions between people based on the groups, classes, or other categories to which they belong or are perceived to belong. People may be discriminated on basis of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation, as well as other categories. Complaints of discrimination may be referred to the CA Civil Rights Department (formerly the CA Department of Fair Employment and Housing).

  • Pesticide Violations

    Pesticide Violation allegations are handled by the local county Agricultural Commissioner’s Office.  To find your local ag commissioner please visit the CDFA Website. For more information on pesticide related claims please visit the California Department of Pesticide Regulation.

Employment and taxes

  • Labor-Peace-Agreement Complaints

    Failure to meet labor-peace-agreement requirements allegations are handled by the California Agricultural Labor Relations Board (ALRB).  These complaints are handled by the ALRB.   

  • Labor and Payment Issues

    Failure to properly pay workers is a matter that is regulated by the California Department of Industrial Relations Division of Labor Standards Enforcement (DLSE).  DLSE adjudicates wage claims on behalf of workers who file claims for nonpayment of wages, overtime, or vacation pay, pursuant to California Labor Code. Visit the DIR website to learn how to file a wage claim.

  • Workplace Safety Issues

    Failure to maintain a safe workplace or provided mandated training allegations are referred to the California Department of Industrial Relation’s Division of Occupational Safety and Health (DOSH), better known as Cal/OSHA is responsible for regulating the protections of California workers and improving workplace health and safety.  Visit the DIR website for more information on how to file a complaint with Cal/OSHA.

Scams

  • Identity Theft Reports

    Report identity theft to the CA Office of the Attorney General.

  • Fraud Reports

    Fraudulent use of a person’s address on a website can be reported to the State of California Department of Justice using their online form for consumer complaints against a business or company.

Taxes

  • Payroll or Employment Tax Complaints

    Payroll or Employment Tax matters are referred to the California Employment Development Department (EDD). Learn more about how to report fraud on the EDD website.

  • Cannabis Excise or Sales Tax Complaints

    Cannabis Excise or Sales Tax matters are referred noted and referred to the California Department Fee and Tax Administration (CDTFA).  Learn how to report suspected tax fraud activity on thee CDTFA website.
