Follow these steps to safeguard yourself, your business, and your employees from criminal activity.

Definitions

Robbery

  • Taking of items by force or fear.
  • Example: bank robbery

Burglary

  • Entering a building with the intent to commit a theft.
  • Example: A late night break-in.

Theft

  • Typically committed by employees.
  • Example: An employee steals cash from a register.

Tips for staying safe

Robbery

  1. Call the police immediately if you see something suspicious.
    • Post law enforcement emergency and non-emergency phone numbers in a visible location.
  2. Maintain visibility into your place of business.
    • Keep windows clear of signs or merchandise to avoid blocking the view into your business
    • Keep all entrances, exits and parking areas well lit.
  3. Protect transactional areas
    • Install physical barriers that are high enough to keep unauthorized persons out of the area where cash transactions take place.
    • Place registers at a safe distance from the door.
  4. Install a hold-up alarm system.
  5. Keep cash on premises to a minimum.
    • Make frequent cash drops.
    • Keep your on-premise safe out of sight and locked at all times.  
    • Use a courier service if you’re dropping cash off premises.
  6. Use more than one person to open and/or close the business.  There is safety in numbers.

Burglary

  1. Secure the premises when closed.
    • Install high-quality locks.
    • Check the condition of the locks on a regular basis.
    • Instruct the last person who leaves the premises to lock all doors and windows. Include this step in your closing instructions. 
  2. Install a security system or burglar alarm.
    • Check your protection system to make sure it is working properly.
    • Have a backup power supply in case of an outage. 
    • Choose a system that includes video surveillance.
  3. Secure valuables.
    • Remove all merchandise at the end of each day.
    • Place merchandise that can’t be removed in a safe.
  4. Secure your safe.
    • Keep the safe at your business out of customer view
    • Bolt the safe to the floor or find another way to prevent it from being moved  
  5. Keep windows clear.
    • Don’t tint or obscure your windows. This allows burglars to act out of sight once they are inside your business.
    • Apply a protective film to the windows to make them difficult to break or remove
  6. Keep your building well-lit.
    • Burglars prefer to work in the dark. Install motion lights on the roof and perimeter of your building to deter suspicious activity.

Theft

Most theft is committed by employees and can be addressed by policies and procedures.

  1. Consider an employee policy of no handbags or backpacks, or only clear bags.
  2. Review your video footage frequently.
  3. Provide each employee with an individual alarm code.
  4. Limit access to your safe.
