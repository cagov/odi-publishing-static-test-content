The Chief of the Law Enforcement Division, for the Department of Cannabis Control has a legal mandate to ensure procedures exist for investigating complaints made by members of the public against Law Enforcement Division personnel.  

Allegations of misconduct against Law Enforcement Division personnel are investigated thoroughly and objectively, and appropriate action taken, with the goals of maintaining the integrity of the Division and the confidence of the public. 

Procedures

Every citizen has the right to make a complaint against any employee of the Law Enforcement Division. The complaint may be made to any supervisor or the Professional Standards Unit. A complaint may be made in person, by telephone, by mail, or by e-mail.  

Once a complaint is received, it is reviewed and either forwarded to the employeesâ€™ supervisor for a management inquiry or forwarded to the Professional Standards Unit for an Internal Affairs investigation. Each allegation is examined and investigated thoroughly. Upon the completion of the investigation, the case will be forwarded to the Chief for final disposition. A closing letter will be sent to the complainant and subject employee outlining the resolution of the complaint within 30 days of final disposition.
